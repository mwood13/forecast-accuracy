# When the Hot-Hand Turns Cold: Responses to Streaks of Inaccurate Forecasts

This repository holds the code and manuscript for this project. 

## Abstract: 

When making decisions involving response and preparation for hurricanes and other disas-
ters, individuals must determine how much risk they are willing to accept, and how much risk
they believe they will face. There are many factors that can drive perceptions of risk. Notable
drivers of risk perception are recency bias, and the hot-hand fallacy. Using the results of past
hurricane forecasts and sales of emergency supplies, I find that responses to hurricane forecasts
are largely influenced by past both recency bias and the hot-hand fallacy. Panic buying is sig-
nificantly larger in counties with inaccurate most recent forecasts, and panic buying continues
to get marginally worse as inaccurate forecasts are repeated.
